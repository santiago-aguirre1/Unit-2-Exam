Microsoft was founded on April 4th, 1975. It was founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen


Microsoft was founded on April 4th , 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Paul Allen was a software engineer at Honeywell when, in 1974, he was walking in Harvard Square near Harvard University when he noticed the cover of January 1975’s edition of Popular Electronics, which featured the world’s first microcomputer. Allen purchased the magazine and hurried to Currier house at Harvard College to show it to his high school classmate Bill Gates. They both saw the potential to develop a BASIC implementation for the system. Gates called Altair’s manufacturer, Micro Instrumentation (MITS) and offered to show the implementation. At the time, neither Allen or Gates had an interpreter for the Altair system. In the eight weeks leading up to the demo, however, they developed a BASIC interpreter with Monte Davidoff’s help. On the day of the demo, Allen arrived in Albuquerque to meet MITS. MITS agreed to distribute the BASIC, and the two moved to Albuquerque. Soon after, Gates left Harvard to join Allen and co-found Microsoft. Microsoft’s revenue in 1976 was $16,005 at the end of the year.
